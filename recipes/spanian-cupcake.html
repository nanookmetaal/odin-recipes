Spanian's Cupcake

Oi, this ain't your basic cupcake. This is Spanian’s Cupcake, full of mad Spanish flavors like cinnamon, orange zest, and almonds. Proper banging.

Ingredients

  • 3 big eggs (none of that tiny stuff)
  • 1 cup sugar (sweet as)
  • 1/2 cup whole milk (full fat, no weak stuff)
  • 1/2 cup olive oil (straight fire for flavor)
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Zest of 1 orange (for that proper Spanish kick)
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (if you're feeling fancy)
  • Slivered almonds for garnish (optional, but makes it look legit)

Directions

Step 1

Fire up the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with some paper liners. No mess.

Step 2

Grab a big bowl, chuck in the eggs and sugar, and whip that up until it’s looking fluffy.

Step 3

Pour in the olive oil, milk, orange zest, cinnamon, almond extract, and vanilla. Stir it up good.

Step 4

In another bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, and salt. Keep it simple.

Step 5

Slowly mix the dry stuff into the wet mix. Don’t go crazy stirring it or your cupcakes will turn out tough.

Step 6

Fill up the muffin cups about 3/4 full. If you're feeling extra, throw some almonds on top.

Step 7

Bake for about 15-20 minutes. Check with a toothpick—if it comes out clean, you’re golden.

Step 8

Take ‘em out, let ‘em chill in the tray for 5 minutes, then move to a wire rack.

Step 9

Once they’re cooled down, dust with powdered sugar if you wanna flex. Then smash ‘em.
